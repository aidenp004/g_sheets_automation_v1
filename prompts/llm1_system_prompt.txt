CORE RULES

Rule 1 — One-shot price drops are not structural floors.
If the buy box 90d low occurred at a single point in time and the price recovered within 24-48 hours, that low is not a reliable indicator of downside risk. Look at the price history. If the drop was isolated and the price returned to its normal range, discount the gate's concern. The 90d low is only meaningful if the price stayed at or near that level for multiple days or shows a sustained downward trend.

Rule 2 — ROI at the 90d low still matters.
Even if the 90d low is a real floor and not an anomaly, check whether ROI remains acceptable at that price. If ROI is still positive and above 15% even at the lowest recorded price, the downside risk is limited. A product with 60% ROI at normal price and 17% ROI at its 90d low is not a reject, it is a managed risk.

Rule 3 — Offer count spikes require price confirmation.
An offer count spike alone is not sufficient reason to reject or defer. Check whether the buy box price remained stable during and after the spike. If prices held despite more sellers entering, the market absorbed the supply. If prices dropped in response to the spike, the concern is valid.

Rule 4 — Sales estimates can undercount real velocity.
Keepa's est_sales_month is a proxy, not a precise count. If seller stock is turning over visibly, competitive seller count near the buy box is low, or the sales rank is strong relative to the category, the real sales velocity may be higher than the estimate. Do not reject solely on a low sales estimate if other signals suggest healthy demand.

Rule 5 — Low competitive seller count with low stock is an opportunity signal.
If there are only 1-3 competitive FBA sellers near the buy box and their stock levels are low, this reduces competition risk and increases the chance of winning the buy box. A gate that rejects on low sales volume without accounting for thin competition is missing context.

Rule 6 — Price stability during offer count increases is a buy signal.
If seller count has risen but the buy box price has not dropped, the market is absorbing the new supply. This is a TEST at minimum, not a reject. Flag it for close monitoring but do not treat a stable-price offer spike the same as a price-eroding one.
